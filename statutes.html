Governance

Statutes

The founding statutes of the Czech & Slovak Union, drafted for adoption by its General Assembly.

🚧

Draft, not yet in force. These statutes are currently under construction and have not yet been formally adopted by the General Assembly. The text below reflects the current working draft and may still change before adoption.

Draft version · 2026

Art. 1: Name, Seat & Duration

Under the name “Czech & Slovak Union” (the “Union”), an association exists within the meaning of Art. 60 et seq. of the Swiss Civil Code (ZGB). The Union has its seat at the University of St. Gallen (HSG), St. Gallen, Switzerland, and is established for an indefinite duration.

Art. 2: Purpose

The Union is a non-profit, politically and religiously neutral student association. Its purposes are to:

  • Bring together Czech and Slovak students at the University of St. Gallen, as well as students and friends of the Union who feel connected to Czech and Slovak culture, language, or traditions;
  • Organize social, cultural, and academic events that foster community and cultural exchange;
  • Support new Czech and Slovak students in their transition to studying and living at HSG;
  • Preserve and share Czech and Slovak traditions and customs within the HSG community; and
  • Build and maintain relationships with sponsors, alumni, and partner organizations in support of the above.

Art. 3: Non-Profit Status

The Union pursues no commercial or profit-making purpose. Any funds and assets of the Union may only be used for purposes consistent with Art. 2. No distributions are made to members.

Art. 4: Membership

4.1 Admission

Membership is open to all students of the University of St. Gallen, regardless of nationality, who support the purpose of the Union. Membership requests may be submitted informally (including by email) and are confirmed by the Board.

4.2 Rights & Duties

Members are entitled to participate in the Union's events and activities, to attend and vote at the General Assembly, and to stand for election to the Board. Members are expected to act in the spirit of the Union's purpose and values.

4.3 Termination

Membership ends upon resignation (by written notice to the Board), upon a member ceasing to be enrolled at HSG for more than two consecutive academic years without renewing their membership, or by exclusion for conduct seriously contrary to the Union's purpose, decided by the Board with a right of appeal to the General Assembly.

Art. 5: Organs of the Union

The organs of the Union are:

  • The General Assembly;
  • The Board; and
  • The Auditor(s), where appointed.

Art. 6: General Assembly

The General Assembly is the supreme organ of the Union. It convenes at least once per academic year, ordinarily in the autumn semester. An extraordinary General Assembly may be convened by the Board or upon written request of at least one-fifth of the members.

The General Assembly is responsible for:

  • Electing and, where necessary, removing members of the Board;
  • Approving the annual report and financial accounts;
  • Setting membership fees, if any;
  • Amending these Statutes; and
  • Resolving on the dissolution of the Union.

Resolutions are passed by a simple majority of members present, except where these Statutes require a qualified majority. Each member present has one vote.

Art. 7: The Board

The Board manages the day-to-day affairs of the Union and represents it towards third parties. It is elected by the General Assembly for a term of one academic year and may be re-elected. The Board consists of at least the following roles:

  • President: overall leadership and external representation of the Union;
  • Vice President: supports the President and deputizes in their absence;
  • Head of Finance: manages the Union's accounts, budget, and financial reporting;
  • Head of Events: plans and organizes the Union's social and cultural events; and
  • Head of Marketing: manages communication, social media, and outreach.

The Board may co-opt additional members or create further roles as needed, subject to ratification at the next General Assembly. The Union is validly bound by the joint signature of two Board members, one of whom must be the President or Vice President.

Art. 8: Finances

The Union's funds derive from membership fees (if any), sponsorships, donations, merchandise sales, and event revenue. The financial year runs in line with the academic year. The Board is responsible for keeping proper accounts, which are presented to the General Assembly for approval.

The liabilities of the Union are covered exclusively by the Union's own assets; personal liability of members is excluded, in accordance with Art. 75a ZGB.

Art. 9: Amendments to the Statutes

These Statutes may be amended by a resolution of the General Assembly passed by a two-thirds majority of members present, provided the proposed amendment was communicated to members in advance of the meeting.

Art. 10: Dissolution

The Union may be dissolved by a resolution of the General Assembly passed by a two-thirds majority of members present. In the event of dissolution, any remaining assets shall be donated to a non-profit organization with a similar purpose, and shall in no case be distributed among the members.

Art. 11: Final Provisions

Matters not addressed in these Statutes are governed by Art. 60 et seq. of the Swiss Civil Code. These Statutes enter into force upon adoption by the Founding General Assembly in 2026.
